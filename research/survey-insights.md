# Primary Research — Zomato Gold User Survey

**Sample:** 71 Zomato Gold users (active, churned, expired) · Delhi NCR · self-administered questionnaire + follow-up conversations.

## Six signals

| # | Finding | Evidence | Root cause | Business impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Solo meals dominate | 74% of orders < ₹500 | Cart thresholds punish the core use case | Forced over-ordering, abandonment |
| 2 | High-frequency base | 71% order weekly or more | Habit exists — loyalty doesn't | High LTV, easily lost |
| 3 | Zero lock-in | 52% run Zomato + Swiggy side-by-side | Every order is re-shopped on final price | Churn is one bad checkout away |
| 4 | Renewal cliff | 45% cancel after 3 months | "Didn't order enough to justify it" | Recurring-revenue leak |
| 5 | Month-2 value drop | Perceived value index 100 → 62 (M1→M2) | Novelty fades before habit forms | Renewal decision is pre-lost |
| 6 | Ecosystem blindness | 65% use delivery perks only | Blinkit/District perks invisible post-purchase | Weak multi-vertical growth |

## Personas derived

1. **Rohan Sharma — The Budget Student** (power user, active): 4–6 orders/wk, AOV ₹150–300; ₹99 minimum forces over-ordering; no student plan.
2. **Anjali Verma — The Busy Professional** (power user, active): 5–7 orders/wk, AOV ₹500–800; lunch-hour delays and hidden fees erode premium trust.
3. **Rahul Singh — The Price Checker** (at-risk, churned to Swiggy One): decides purely on final checkout price; generic discounts create zero loyalty.
4. **Sneha Kapoor — The Dining Explorer** (at-risk, active): wants experiences, not just discounts; District and dining perks invisible to her.

## How the survey informed prioritization

- **Reach** estimates in the RICE model come from persona sizes in the sample.
- **Confidence** scores reflect whether a bet is validated by two or more methods (survey + journey/benchmark).
- The ecosystem bet ranked #1 because it touches every persona weekly (groceries) instead of a segment occasionally.
